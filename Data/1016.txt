James Parks At the Cathedral Church of St. John the Divine in New York yesterday Mary Windels, a daughter of Albert Mclvaine Mitchell of Warren, Pa., and the late Carolyn Davidson Mitchell, was married to David Herron Meese, a son of the late Mr. and Mrs. Alfred Hall Meese of Schenectady, N.Y. The Very Rev. James Parks Morton, dean of the cathedral, performed the ceremony. The bride, who will keep her name professionally, is a staff associate of the city hospital visiting committee of the United Hospital Fund in New York. She graduated from Goucher College. Her previous marriage ended in divorce, as did the bridegroom's. Her father, who is retired, was an executive vice president of the Struthers Wells Corporation, a manufacturer of heat-exchange equipment in Warren. Mr. Meese is a systems analyst in the office systems planning and technical education department of the International Business Machines Corporation in Purchase, N.Y. He graduated from the University of Rochester and received a master's degree in music from its Eastman School of Music.
