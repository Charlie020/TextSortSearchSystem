Reception for Peace Jan. 6 - Leonard Bernstein, George Will, Henry Kissinger, Jason Robards, Roberta Peters, Kitty Carlisle Hart, Robert Morgenthau, Ellen Burstyn, James McCracken, Misha Dichter and Shelley Winters are among the luminaries expected to attend a reception at Tavern on the Green honoring Elie Wiesel, winner of the 1986 Nobel Peace Prize. The 7:45 P.M. gathering will benefit Writers and Artists for Peace in the Middle East. Tickets, $200, from 212-421-1950. Patriotic Debuts Jan. 9 - The Colonial Debutante Ball, an annual event since 1934, will be held at the Pierre starting with cocktails at 7:30 P.M. The event will benefit the scholarship fund of the New York Chapter of the Sons of the American Revolution. White tie preferred. Tickets, $110, from 212-206-1776. Uptown Readings Jan. 14 - Founded in 1958 as an after-school study group in a housing project, the East Harlem Tutorial Program has expanded to year-round programs of one-to-one remedial work in reading, writing and math for 500 youngsters at a time. The program is privately financed, dependent on sources such as its annual fund-raising event, which will be held this year at the Mount Sinai Hospital's Stern Auditorium on Fifth Avenue at 100th Street. George Plimpton will be the host as the writers Maya Angelou, Kurt Vonnegut Jr., Gail Lumet Buckley and Calvin Trillin read from their works. A reception with the writers follows the 8 P.M. readings. Tickets, $35 or $10 for students and local residents, from 212-831-0650. Medical Get-Together Jan. 17 - Two volunteer groups of the Albert Einstein College of Medicine of Yeshiva University, the young men's division and the Westchester women's division, are holding a black-tie dinner-dance at the Plaza starting with 7:30 P.M. cocktails. Proceeds will aid a $90 million fund drive to support research projects, student aid, a faculty scholars program and the establishment of an institute for molecular medicine. Tickets, $200, from 212-430-4241. In Hartford Jan. 17 - A preview of ''J. Pierpont Morgan, Collector: European Decorative Arts from the Wadsworth Atheneum'' along with refreshments and dancing in the great hall of the museum in Hartford will be held from 8 P.M. to midnight. Exhibition open to the public Jan. 18 through March 15. Tickets for museum benefit, $15 for members, $20 for nonmembers, from 203-278-2670. For Big Brothers . . . Jan. 20 - Of the 400 Big Brother agencies around the country that help children from troubled or single- parent homes by matching them with trustworthy older adults for friendship and counseling, the New York branch is the oldest. Founded in 1904, the largest fund raising event for Big Brothers of New York is its annual dinner, which will be held this year at the Waldorf-Astoria starting with cocktails at 6:30 P.M. Tickets, $300, from 212-686-2042. . . . and the Girl Scouts Jan. 20 - The St. Regis-Sheraton Roof will be the setting for the annual Winter Festival for the benefit of the Girl Scout Council of Greater New York. The black-tie evening starts with 7 P.M. cocktails followed by dinner, dancing and entertainment. Tickets, $250, from 212-645-4000. Concert Benefit Jan. 20 - Mozart, Strauss and Beethoven will be on the program, Erich Leinsdorf on the podium and the New York Philharmonic on stage at a benefit concert for the Jewish National Fund's New Leadership of Greater New York. The New Leadership is a group of young business and professional people organized to support the fund's ecological development of Israel. A 6 P.M. cocktail party precedes the 7:30 concert at Avery Fisher Hall. Tickets, $100, from 212-737-7441. SOCIAL EVENTS
