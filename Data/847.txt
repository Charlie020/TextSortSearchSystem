DARRELL WALTRIP, veteran stock car racer, unofficially drove the fastest lap in the history of Daytona International Speedway's 2.5-mile tri-oval yesterday, turning 207.517 miles an hour in his Waddell Wilson-prepared Chevrolet. Waltrip, clocked at 43.37 seconds in the Tide Chevrolet, went nearly 2.5 m.p.h. faster than Bill Elliott's 205.114 m.p.h. lap in qualifying for the 1985 Daytona 500. Official Nascar records can only be set during a qualifying session for a race, according to Larry Balewski, the track public relations director. The testing yesterday was the second of a three-day session for Hendrick Motorsports in preparation for the Feb. 15 running of the 29th annual Daytona 500. SPORTS PEOPLE
