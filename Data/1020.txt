Maryellen Youngs of Mentor, Ohio, and Richard Lauretig of Cleveland, was married yesterday to William E. Hammond, the son of the late Mr. and Mrs. William K. Hammond of New York and Greenwich, Conn. The bride's mother, a Unity Church of Christianity minister, performed the ceremony at the Unity Center of Practical Christianity in Manhattan. The bride, who will retain her name, is the supervisor of education services at The Door, a nonprofit agency for inner-city youths in New York, and the founder and director of Camp Sun-Up and All-Ways English, schools in Caracas, Venezuela. She graduated from Ohio University. Her father, now retired, was a salesman for the S. & W. Food Company in Cleveland. Mr. Hammond is a financial consultant in New York. He attended Le Rosey School in Rolle, Switzerland, and graduated from Phillips Academy in Andover, Mass., and Yale University and received an M.B.A. degree from Columbia University. His father was the budget director of the Melville Shoe Corporation in New York.
