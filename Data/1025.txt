At Christ Episcopal Church in Alexandria, Va., yesterday Mary Ann Ballard, a daughter of Mr. and Mrs. John Edward Ballard of Chatham and Mantoloking, N.J., was married to Edwin Michael Ward Jr., the son of the Rev. and Mrs. Ward of Alexandria. The ceremony was performed by the bridegroom's father and the Rev. Mark Anschutz, rector of the church. Kimberly Ballard Plechash was her sister's matron of honor. John R. Rhinelander was the best man. Mrs. Ward until recently was an account executive for Earle Palmer Brown, an advertising agency in Bethesda, Md. She and Mr. Ward graduated from Duke University, she cum laude and he magna cum laude. Mr. Ward was elected to Phi Beta Kappa. The bride's father is a staff manager for the American Telephone & Telegraph Company in Bedminster, N.J. Mr. Ward is an alumnus of the Groton School and a candidate for an M.B.A. degree at the University of Chicago. His father is the headmaster of St. Stephen's Episcopal School for Boys in Alexandria and former headmaster of the Salisbury School.
